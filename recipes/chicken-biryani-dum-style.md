\## Ingredients

\- Chicken

\- Birista (Fried Onions): 1 cup, crushed

\- Bombay Biryani Masala: Half packet

\- Salt: 1 tablespoon

\- Tomatoes: 2, sliced

\- Green Chilies: 8, slit

\- Yogurt (Dahi): 1 cup, beaten

\- Lemon Juice: 1 lime

\- Mint (Podina)

\- Coriander (Kothmir): Chopped

\- Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tablespoon

\- Oil: Half cup

\- Basmati Rice: At least 3 cups

\- Water: 6 cups

\- Salt: 1 tablespoon

\- Shah Zeera (Caraway Seeds): 1 teaspoon

\- Whole Spices: Cinnamon, 2 Cloves, 4 Black Peppercorns, 4 Bay Leaves

\- Ghee (Clarified Butter): 6 tablespoons

\- Saffron (Zaffran): A little



\## Steps

1\. In a large pot, mix chicken, crushed birista, biryani masala, salt, tomatoes, green chilies, yogurt, lemon juice, mint, coriander, ginger-garlic paste, and oil. Mix well with hands. Level and keep aside with a heavy object on top.

2\. In a separate pot, boil rice with water, salt, shah zeera, and whole spices until 70% cooked.

3\. Drain all water from rice using a sieve.

4\. Spread the rice over the marinated chicken layer in the first pot.

5\. Level the rice. Pour 6 tablespoons of ghee and sprinkle saffron on top.

6\. Cover the pot tightly with foil paper.

7\. Place a tawa on the stove. Put the biryani pot on top of the tawa and cook on a very slow flame for 40 minutes.

8\. After 40 minutes, check for steam/smoke. Turn off the gas but keep the pot covered for another 20 minutes before serving.
